Why is it disastrous?

Plastics made up of organic compounds or synthetic materials, do not decompose easily and can get into the soil and leach toxic chemicals. They eventually break down the soil which is a source of food for many animals and in turn, killing them.
  1. For marine animals, plastic breaks down into millions of microparticles that easily enter the mouths of smaller fish. These plastics fill their stomach and cause suffocation, starvation and drowning.
  2. Incineration of plastic releases harmful, toxic and carcinogenic pollutants into the air. Breathing this can cause respiratory ailments, stress human ailments and can kill a person. 
  3. Bisphenol A (BPA) is a harmful substance constituting plastic which is used to make millions of plastic food and beverage containers, protective linings,etc. can alter the transport, function, action and production of hormones. It can even cause cancer.

How can you help?

Reuse, Reduce, Recycle


Currently, only 9% of the world's plastic is being recycled. Most of the plastics manufactured are created for the purpose of single-use and this is a complete waste. Even though the goal may be far away, small efforts can make a big difference.

Single-use plastics consist of plastic bags, water bottles, straws, cups, utensils, dry cleaning bags, take-out containers and others that are immediately discarded once used. One way is to refuse the need for single-use plastics which are not needed such as takeout utensils, plastic bags, etc. You can purchase reusable products such as grocery bags, produce bags, bottles, utensils, coffee cups, and dry cleaning garment bags.

Use recycled plastic and other materials such as brown paper bags, paper straws, grocery bags, etc. Use plastic bottles to store water instead of purchasing new containers. Carry a water bottle around. Upto 20 billion plastic water bottles are thrown every year.

There are 7 different compositions of plastics. These can help everyone make more informed decisions. Each type categorises the plastic into its composition and the right method to dispose it:

 

PETE or PET: Soda bottles, Water bottles, Medicine jars, Salad dressing bottles, Peanut butter jars, Combs, Ropes and Carpets. These must be disposed in bins displaying PET. They are all recyclable.


HDPE: Juice containers, Grocery bags, Trash bags, Shampoo and conditioner bottles, Soap bottles, Detergent containers, Bleach containers and Toys. These must be disposed in bins displaying HDPE. They are all recyclable.


PVC: Plumbing pipes, Grocery bags, Shoes, Window frames and Sewage pipes. These must be disposed in bins displaying PVC. Ask your recycler whether they can be recycled.


LDPE: Sandwich bags, Squeezable bottles such as those for honey, ketchup, etc. Frozen food bags, Grocery bags and Flexible container lids. These must be disposed in bins displaying LDPE. Ask your recycler whether they can be recycled.

PP: Tupperware, Kitchenware, Yogurt containers, Prescription bottles, Bottle caps, Take-out containers and disposable cups and plates. These must be disposed in bins displaying PP. They are not recyclable.


PS:  Disposable coffe cups, Plastic food boxes, Plastic cutlery and Packing foam. These must be disposed in bins displaying PS. They are not recyclable.


N/A: Plastic CDs and DVDs, Baby bottles, Medical storage containers and every other product. Ask your recycler whether they can be recycled.
